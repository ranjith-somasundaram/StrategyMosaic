Common responses after the opening hook — and how to steer without losing momentum.

"We already have a data warehouse — isn't that the same thing?"▼
A warehouse stores data. Mosaic defines what it means — and makes that definition available to every tool that consumes it. The warehouse is still there doing its job. Mosaic is the intelligence layer on top — the reason "revenue" means the same thing in Tableau as it does in Power BI and in your AI agent. Most of our customers have Snowflake or Databricks already. Mosaic sits on top of it.
"We're all-in on Microsoft — Fabric handles this for us."▼
Fabric is strong within Microsoft — but the moment a team opens Tableau, connects via Python, or uses an AI tool outside Microsoft, you lose consistency. Mosaic is cloud and tool agnostic — it works alongside Fabric, not against it. The pricing model is also predictable versus Fabric's consumption-based cost, which spikes as adoption scales.
"Our Snowflake / Databricks costs aren't really a concern right now."▼
Fair — cost often becomes the conversation as BI adoption scales and more dashboards get built. The other angle that typically resonates is the consistency piece — if teams are using multiple tools or exploring AI, Mosaic ensures everyone works from the same certified numbers. Which of those is more relevant to where you are today?
"We're exploring AI but haven't connected it to our data yet."▼
That's the best time to have this conversation. Companies that deploy AI on ungoverned data end up with hallucinated outputs at scale — which is harder to fix than starting right. Mosaic is the AI-readiness layer. Get the governed foundation in place first, and every AI initiative — agents, chatbots, predictive models — lands on trusted data from day one. We support ChatGPT, Claude, Copilot, and Gemini through the same layer via MCP.
"What's the commercial model — is this expensive?"▼
Mosaic is seat-based — not compute-based. The cost doesn't spike as query volume grows. And given customers typically see around 40% reduction in warehouse compute costs, the net cost of adding Mosaic is often neutral or better from day one. Happy to run a quick TCO estimate if that would be useful for an internal conversation.
"We use dbt for our semantic layer already."▼
dbt is excellent at transformation — but its semantic layer is warehouse-bound and code-first. Business teams can't use it directly without engineering. Mosaic turns dbt outputs into a business-friendly layer accessible to any tool, any AI, any user — with governance and lineage built in. They're complementary, not competing.
Do this on the opening call
  • Lead with a specific pain — cost, consistency, or AI — based on what you know about the prospect
  • Use plain language: "trusted data layer", "one version of the numbers", "sits on top of what you have"
  • Name tools they likely use — Snowflake, Power BI, Excel — to make it concrete and specific
  • Pause after 30 seconds and check in before going further
  • Bring up compute cost savings early — fastest path to a second meeting
  • Offer a TCO model as a tangible next step — converts curiosity into a meeting
  • Ask one question and let them talk — their answer shapes the rest of the call
Avoid on the opening call
  • Don't say "universal semantic layer" — say "one trusted data layer that everything reads from"
  • Don't list Studio, Sentinel, and connectors all at once before they ask
  • Don't pitch for more than 30–40 seconds without checking in with a question
  • Don't go competitive in the opening — anchor value first, competitive later
  • Don't fill silence after the hook — let it land and wait for their reaction
  • Don't lead with features — lead with their cost or consistency pain
  • Don't ask multiple questions at once — one question, then listen fully
