Stack & technology pushback
"We already have a data warehouse — isn't that the same thing?"▼
A warehouse stores data. Mosaic defines what it means — and makes that definition available to every tool that consumes it. The warehouse stays exactly where it is. Mosaic is the intelligence layer on top — the reason "revenue" means the same thing in Tableau as it does in Power BI and in your AI agent. Most of our customers have Snowflake or Databricks already. Mosaic sits on top of it, it doesn't replace it.
"We're all-in on Microsoft — Fabric handles this for us."▼
Fabric is strong within the Microsoft ecosystem — but the moment a team opens Tableau, connects via Python, or uses an AI tool outside Microsoft, you lose consistency. Mosaic is cloud and tool agnostic — it works alongside Fabric, not against it. And Fabric's consumption-based pricing spikes as adoption scales. Mosaic is user-based — predictable regardless of query volume growth.
"We already have Power BI's built-in semantic model."▼
Power BI's semantic model is great — inside Power BI. The moment anyone in Tableau, Excel, or an AI agent asks the same question, they're working from a different definition. Mosaic makes your certified metric definitions available to all of those tools simultaneously — DAX for Power BI, SQL for Tableau, REST for AI agents. One definition, every tool, not just one.
"We use dbt for our semantic layer already."▼
dbt is excellent at transformation — but its semantic layer is warehouse-bound and code-first. Business teams can't use it without engineering involvement, and it doesn't serve metrics directly to BI tools or AI agents. Mosaic turns dbt outputs into a business-friendly layer accessible to any tool, any AI, any user — with governance and lineage built in. They're complementary, not competing.
"We have a data catalogue — Collibra or Alation. Same thing?"▼
Very different roles. A data catalogue documents what data exists and its lineage — it's a metadata directory. Mosaic is a computation and governance layer. It doesn't just describe your metrics, it serves them — live and consistently — to any BI tool or AI agent that asks, while enforcing row-level and object-level security across every access point through Mosaic Sentinel. Think of the catalogue as the dictionary; Mosaic is the engine that enforces definitions and governs access at query time. Most customers use both.
"Our data is on-prem — can Mosaic even work with that?"▼
Yes — Mosaic connects to 200+ sources including on-prem databases, and it supports hybrid environments. It queries your on-prem data where it lives without requiring you to move it to the cloud first. It also supports air-gapped environments using offline LLM models for the AI features. The cloud requirement is for the Mosaic service layer itself, not for your underlying data sources.

And through Mosaic Sentinel, every AI interaction is tracked with full lineage, so you know exactly how the answer was derived.

  • Mosaic's in-memory engine reduces Databricks compute spend — often paying for Mosaic through the savings it generates on the Databricks bill.

  • True vendor-agnostic governance and portability. Your business logic lives in Mosaic, not inside a vendor's platform.
Denodo
Data virtualisation▼
  • Denodo focuses on data federation — live querying across sources. Its semantic layer is not the product — it's a feature sitting inside SQL views with no independent existence.
  • In Denodo, KPIs live inside SQL views. In Mosaic, metrics are first-class certified objects — with lineage, governance, and AI-readiness built in from the ground up.
  • When an AI agent or MCP client queries Denodo, it queries a SQL view. When it queries Mosaic, it resolves against a certified metric definition with business context, security, and full auditability.
  • Denodo's primary cache uses an external JDBC database — not in-memory. Mosaic's in-memory engine is significantly faster for complex cross-source analytics.
  • Mosaic adds AI-readiness, business logic, non-technical self-service, and Sentinel governance. Denodo's semantic layer is a feature. Mosaic's is the entire product.
Cost angle: Denodo's federation model still hits source systems for every live query with no intelligent in-memory layer. Mosaic's caching and hybrid execution protect warehouse compute costs in a way Denodo's architecture structurally cannot.

Data virtualisation▼

  • Mosaic sits on top of Starburst — giving the business certified metrics, governed access, and AI-readiness on top of Starburst's query speed.
  • Enterprise-wide metric consistency, self-service for non-technical users, and AI agent integration are capabilities Starburst doesn't address.

  • No native BI consumption connectors, no AI agent integration, no row-level security enforcement across tools, no governance platform equivalent to Sentinel.
  • Mosaic turns dbt outputs into a business-friendly universal semantic layer — accessible to any tool, any AI, any user. They're complementary, not competing.
Cost angle: dbt Cloud is an additional consumption cost layer on top of your warehouse. Mosaic's user-based model covers the entire semantic layer, BI connectivity, and AI agent integration in one predictable cost.
