03How We Win vs Denodo — 4 key differentiators

These are the four messages to lead with when Denodo is already in the conversation. The goal is to shift the discussion from data access to semantic control, trusted AI, stack simplification, and execution efficiency.

01 / Core Architecture
Consistent Meaning vs. Disciplined SQL
Denodo
Governs access through SQL views. “Net Revenue” lives as code inside views and consistency depends on teams reusing the same logic.
Mosaic
Governs meaning through certified semantic objects. The same definition is reused across dashboards, applications, and AI agents.
Why we win
We turn metric consistency into architecture. Denodo relies on discipline and governance meetings. Mosaic makes the same business definition travel everywhere by design.
02 / AI Trust
AI Hallucination Protection at the Source
Denodo
AI agents reason over schemas and views. Reliability depends on whether the right views are modeled and selected.
Mosaic
AI operates on governed semantic definitions in the Universal Semantic Layer, not raw tables or loosely governed SQL artifacts.
Why we win
Denodo gives AI a pipe to data. Mosaic gives AI governed business meaning. That is the difference between querying data and delivering trusted answers.
03 / Stack Simplification
The Complete “Last Mile”
Denodo
Stops at the data layer. Reporting and dashboards still require separate BI products, integration work, and another governance surface.
Mosaic
Extends from semantic layer to native BI and AI through Strategy One, while still supporting external BI tools when needed.
Why we win
We reduce tool sprawl and close the last mile. One platform governs the path from source data to dashboard to AI interaction.
04 / Performance & Cost
Execution Efficiency vs. Source Dependency
Denodo
Pushdown-heavy architecture means performance and cost remain tied to source systems, with external caching layered on top.
Mosaic
Hybrid execution combines pushdown with an in-memory engine that absorbs repeated and high-concurrency workloads without re-triggering source compute.
Why we win
We decouple usage from warehouse pain. Repeated BI and AI workloads can be served from memory, improving stability while protecting Snowflake and Databricks spend.
