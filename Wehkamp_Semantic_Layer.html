Where the conversation broke down

What Wehkamp wanted — and how Strategy can respond

The final objection was less about whether Mosaic delivered value, and more about whether it matched the engineering shape Wehkamp wanted. Here is each concern — and the direct response.

Automatic, usage-driven optimisation System should learn from query patterns and adapt — no upfront caching or aggregation decisions.
Mosaic's Query Acceleration Engine provides pushdown processing, intelligent caching, and cross-source calculation — usage-aware, not pre-configured. The AI Sync for Linked Models (Feb 2026) further automates maintenance across connected semantic models.
Engineering-friendly workflows Support for YAML/SQL-based work, Git versioning, and bulk operations aligned to how the team already works.
Mosaic Studio supports code-based model creation alongside its AI-powered tooling, including YAML/SQL-based workflows. Engineers are not restricted to a GUI-only paradigm, and the AI assistant for Python query generation automates much of the technical integration work.
Low operating burden for a team of < 5 No new heavy platform footprint requiring a dedicated admin function.
Mosaic Studio accelerates model creation up to 10x with AI assistance — the engineering burden is front-loaded during model creation, not ongoing. The AI assistant handles Python query generation automatically, reducing day-to-day operational effort.
Reuse of existing Databricks structure — no rebuild Leverage their already well-modelled Unity Catalog / Delta Lake structure without starting over.
Mosaic connects directly to Databricks as a data source. It does not replace the medallion architecture — it sits above it. The gold layer becomes Mosaic's input, not a layer to redesign.
"Another platform layer" concern Even when strategically valuable, a second major platform can feel like added operating surface.
The right frame is separation of concerns — not added overhead. Unity Catalog governs data. Mosaic governs business meaning. These are different jobs. Engineers who have lived through logic scattered across Tableau datasets, SSAS cubes, and Excel formulas understand why they belong in different layers.
High transparency into memory, refresh, and cache behaviour Clear visibility into what is accelerated, model state, and performance behaviour.
Mosaic Sentinel's Usage Insights module provides visibility into semantic layer utilisation, consumption patterns, most-used models, and performance data — without data movement or vendor lock-in.

Risks and reservations

Both options have real strengths and real trade-offs

A balanced decision needs both sides stated clearly. These are the risks, honestly assessed.

Risks with a Databricks-only direction

  • The solution may align more strongly to engineering preference than to downstream business-consumption redesign.
  • Tableau and Excel friction may remain partially unresolved even if governance is improved upstream.
  • The daily Excel manipulation issue for 150+ users may persist unless specifically addressed outside the data platform.
  • MSAS retirement requires additional design work outside the main semantic layer decision.
  • The semantic story remains stronger for the platform team than for business-user adoption.
  • AI agents querying Databricks directly carry hallucination and governance risks without a semantic layer between them.

Risks with a Databricks + Strategy direction

  • Wehkamp may perceive another platform layer as overhead if the value is not framed clearly as complementarity, not replacement.
  • The team may perceive Mosaic as requiring more explicit semantic modeling decisions than the fully automated model they prefer.
  • Operational acceptance depends on proving the platform remains manageable for a team of fewer than five engineers.
  • Performance expectations need to be explained carefully to avoid mismatch with system-driven optimisation preferences.
  • Without a clear role split (Databricks governs data; Mosaic governs business meaning), the architecture can be misunderstood as overlap rather than complementarity.

Final recommendation

Databricks + Strategy is the more complete answer to the problem Wehkamp walked in with.

If Wehkamp is optimising primarily for native engineering alignment and minimum architectural change, a Databricks-only direction will feel more natural. It is the right choice if engineering simplicity is the single deciding criterion.

If Wehkamp is optimising for the full enterprise problem it originally defined — semantic consistency across tools, elimination of Excel manipulation for 150+ users, governed downstream consumption, and a credible MSAS modernisation path — then Databricks plus Strategy is the more comprehensive solution.

The strongest Strategy position is not to argue against Databricks as the data platform. It is to show that Databricks and Strategy together can solve more of the real business problem than a Databricks-only semantic strategy.

The verdict: Databricks governs your data. Mosaic governs your business meaning. These are different jobs — and both need to be done for Wehkamp to retire MSAS, solve the Excel problem, and build an AI-ready analytics operating model.
